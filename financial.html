        About Financial Assistance

        Financial assistance plays a crucial role in realizing dreams of overseas education. Edu-Wire Consultancy offers guidance to students regarding financial assistance and education loans. We have a thorough insight about all the national banks offering loans at affordable rates. Financial assistance forms an important aspect of our service to all our registered student clients.
        Studying abroad is an expensive affair and students need financial assistance to cover up all expenses. Admission into an advanced degree program in a foreign university requires large sums of money. Once enrolled, students or their parents are expected to bear not only the cost of college fees, but also other expenses like food, accommodation, travel, stationeries etc. Since universities mainly offer scholarships to students on merit basis, majority of the times parents or students are required to support their education on their own.

        Financial Assistance FAQs

        What types of financial assistance do you offer? +
        We provide guidance on scholarships, education loans, payment plans, and sponsorship opportunities to help fund your studies abroad.
        Can you help me find scholarships? +
        Yes, we help identify suitable scholarships based on your academic background, destination country, and chosen course.
        Do you assist with the education loan process? +
        Absolutely. We work with leading banks and financial institutions to guide you through loan eligibility, documentation, and approval.
        What documents are required for an education loan? +
        Typically, documents include admission letter, fee structure, academic records, income proof, ID proof, and collateral documents (if required).
        Is a co-applicant necessary for a student loan? +
        In most cases, yes. A co-applicant (usually a parent or guardian) with a steady income is required to support the loan application.
        What is the repayment period for an education loan? +
        The repayment period usually ranges from 5 to 15 years and typically begins after the course ends or after a grace period of 6–12 months.
        Are there any zero-interest financial support options? +
        Some universities and government schemes may offer zero or low-interest financial aid. We help you explore and apply for such options.
        Can part-time work help cover living expenses abroad? +
        Yes. Most student visas allow part-time work (typically 20 hours/week), which can help manage basic expenses while studying abroad.
        Do you provide any EMI options for consultancy fees? +
        Yes. We offer flexible EMI plans to make consultancy services more accessible and affordable for students and parents.
        Can I apply for financial aid before getting admission? +
        While most financial aids require admission proof, we can help you pre-assess your eligibility and prepare necessary documentation in advance.
